## 3. The one warning before you try GStack

**GStack is a *methodology* dressed as a tool. The methodology is Gary Tan's.**

That's not bad — it's just the trade. Specifically:

- **Auto-plan ships *Gary's* recommendations** — *(16:31)* He says: "These are sort of programmed to be what I would do if I were you." If your taste, stack, or priorities don't match a YC partner's, the defaults will fight you. Plan to override them.
- **You'll feel the friction before you feel the value** — Office hours forces you through 6 questions before you write a line of code. That's great for new ideas. It is **wrong** if you already know what you're building and just want to ship.
- **Conductor lock-in** — *(02:26)* "The best way to get started with GStack is actually Conductor." Every demo in the video is in Conductor. If you're a vanilla Claude Code user, what you see ≠ what you get.
- **Demo is a fresh project, not a real codebase** — *(04:44)* "This is a fresh project with an initial commit." The opening pitch is "the model wanders, guesses on your data" — but he never shows GStack tackling an unfamiliar 50K-LOC repo. Open question whether the workflow holds up there.
- **Parallel-session cost** — 10–15 simultaneous Claude Code sessions is a real Anthropic bill. Acceptable for YC's president; budget-relevant for a solo bootstrapper.

**Bottom line**: install it for new ideas. Don't drop it on a mature codebase expecting magic. And expect to spend a weekend rewriting the auto-plan defaults to match your style.
